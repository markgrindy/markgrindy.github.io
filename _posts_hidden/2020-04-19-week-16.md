I put myself at increased risk of stress fracturing my foot: <br/>&bull; wore new and different kinds of shoes <br/>&bull; ran on new surfaces, uneven terrain <br/>&bull; increased training load<br/><br/>Seems these can take a few weeks to fully develop and become noticeable, so it&rsquo;s likely the accumulation of these three things that did me in. I&rsquo;ve done even higher volume than I&rsquo;ve been doning, so that&rsquo;s very unlikely to be the culprit. And my goal is to run on trail, so even if that is a cause, I wouldn&rsquo;t have done anything differently. It&rsquo;s the shoes I have control over, and that I probably should have controlled differently.<br/><br/>For now, must avoid reproducing the pain. Reevaluate in 2-3 weeks. Brian says bones take six weeks to heal, so I&rsquo;m thinking no training runs until then. Light jogs may be appropriate before then. We&rsquo;ll see.
