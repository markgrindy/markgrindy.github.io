A little about me:

I was born in Duluth, Minnesota, and raised there by one union member, one manager, one younger brother, and five foreign exchange students. I traveled a circuitous route back to Minnesota, where my wife and I now live.

I spent the decade after leaving the University of Minnesota working with executives, entertainers, athletes, and political leaders from home bases in Washington, D.C., and Chicago, and the Twin Cities. During that time, I served on the re-election campaign of President Barack Obama, where I helped community leaders and national figures alike shape and share their ideas through speeches, op-eds, and other statements offered in support of the President's campaign.

Today, I work alongside the best minds of my generation at WWW. We partner with leaders of all kinds to help them find their story, advance their goals, and make a powerful argument for action—through every format from toasts to keynotes, workshops to white papers, and blog posts to books.

At every opportunity, I earnestly extol the merits of Minnesota. I've done so in word, in deed, and in [the pages](https://www.wsj.com/articles/minnesota-embraces-its-terrible-weather-wont-you-too-1516582494) of the Wall Street Journal. I agree with [the observation](http://collections.mnhs.org/MNHistoryMagazine/articles/34/v34i02p067-078.pdf) made in 1871 by the honorable Kentucky Rep. J. Proctor Knott regarding "the climate of Duluth, unquestionably the most salubrious and delightful to be found anywhere on the Lord's earth."
